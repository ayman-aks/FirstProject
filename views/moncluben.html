National resistance

The national struggle behind the creation of the Wydad Athletic Club

The origin of the creation of the Wydad Athletic Club, lies in the resistance to the oppression of the protectorate. Thus, long before independence, the port of Casablanca was surrounded by a large number of swimming pools, and to be able to have access to these swimming pools, it was necessary to belong to a club, clubs which were created and managed by Europeans. From 1935/1936, a certain number of Moroccan Jews and Muslims were able to join these clubs and thus enjoy the swimming pools of the city. But quickly the number of Moroccans became too important which caused a certain form of fear on the part of the Europeans who dismissed the 'natives' from the clubs. This is how the idea of ​​the creation of a club came about. Moroccans, so as to allow Jews and Muslims to bathe in the swimming pools of the port. Thus came the idea of ​​creating the Wydad club with the avowed objective of participating in Water Polo competitions ...

100% Moroccan Club

The difficulty of creating a 100% Moroccan club during the protectorate

All was not very easy, because after having asked several times for authorization from the French authorities for the creation of the club, requests which were each time unanswered, the Wydadis of the first hour (Haj Mohamed Benjelloun and doctor haj Abdellatif Benjelloun Touimi ), contacted the Franco-Moroccan association to try to resolve the problem of the club's registration. This is how the general resident Nogues intervened personally to authorize the creation of Wydad, this under certain very restrictive conditions: estrangement from religion (no religion in sport), estrangement from politics, no racism (towards the French), sharing of the 12 committee seats between Moroccans and French on an equal basis. Thus on May 8, 1937 for the first time in Morocco, a club was created by Moroccans.

The choice of the name Wydad

Several versions circulate according to the origin of the name of the Casablanca club Wydad. Wydad in Arabic meaning Love, each historian provides his explanation. But that reported by Ahmed Lahrizi in his book on Wydad volume 1, is the most widespread and the most plausible. So according to the latter, during the frequent meetings that took place before the creation of the club, one of the founding members arrived late, the reason being that he was watching the last film of the Egyptian actress and singer Oum Kaltoum, whose title is: "Wydad". Thus came the idea to the founders to give the name of the club, the name of the film ...
